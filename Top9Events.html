    Float the Bow

    Floating on the Bow River in Calgary offers a refreshing and scenic way to experience the city's natural beauty. Participants typically rent inflatable rafts, tubes, or kayaks and leisurely float along the river, taking in views of downtown Calgary, lush riverbanks, and nearby parks.

    Calgary Stampede

    The Calgary Stampede is one of the largest and most iconic rodeo and western festivals in the world, held annually. Attracts over a million visitors each year. It features a wide array of events and attractions, including professional rodeo competitions, chuckwagon races, agricultural exhibits, live music concerts, carnival rides, and a variety of food vendors offering everything from traditional western fare to international cuisine, parades, fireworks display, and cultural showcases.

    Calgary Stampede Official Site Link

    4th Street Lilac Festival

    The Lilac Festival in Calgary is an annual event held in late May that celebrates the arrival of spring and showcases the city's vibrant community spirit. It typically features live music, food vendors, artisans, and various family-friendly activities along 4th Street SW, which is transformed into a bustling pedestrian promenade for the day. The festival's centerpiece is the blooming of thousands of lilac bushes that line the street

    The zipline at Canada Olympic Park offers an exhilarating experience for adventure seekers visiting. Located at the site of the 1988 Winter Olympics, the zipline allows participants to soar above the park's iconic ski jumps and stunning mountain scenery. The experience provides breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape while delivering an adrenaline rush.

    Polar Bear Exhibit at the Calgary Zoo

    The polar bear exhibit at the Calgary Zoo provides visitors with an immersive experience into the Arctic habitat of these magnificent creatures that opened in late 2023. It features a spacious enclosure designed to resemble the natural environment of polar bears, complete with rocky terrain, pools for swimming, and ample space for exploration. Visitors can observe polar bears up close as they swim, play, and interact with each other.

    Official Calgary Zoo Link

  • Activity/Entertainment/Food/Shopping

    Heritage Park

    Heritage Park in Calgary is a living history museum that offers visitors a glimpse into Western Canada's past. Spread over 127 acres, the park features over 180 exhibits and attractions, including historical buildings, costumed interpreters, and vintage vehicles. Visitors can explore various aspects of pioneer life, from the early days of fur trading and indigenous cultures to the settlement of Western Canada in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Highlights include a working steam train, a recreated frontier town with shops and businesses, a First Nations encampment, and a vintage midway with rides and games.

    Official Heritage Park Link

  • Activity/Entertainment

    Butterfield Acres

    Butterfield Acres in Calgary is a family-friendly farmyard experience that offers hands-on interaction with farm animals and outdoor activities. It's designed to provide children with a fun and educational experience in a farm setting. Visitors can participate in various activities such as feeding and petting farm animals like goats, sheep, pigs, and chickens. There are also pony rides, tractor-pulled hayrides, and opportunities to learn about agriculture and animal care through interactive demonstrations and guided tours.
